Hello! My name is Merve Keskin. I am a Postdoctoral Research Scholar at the School of Politics and Global Studies at Arizona State University. My research investigates peace and conflict, political violence, and human rights with a focus on three main areas: 1) the strategic decision-making of non-state armed groups, 2) the causes and impacts of local agreements between non-state armed groups and civilian communities in civil wars, and 3) the international dimensions of civil wars, including the role of sanctions and international interventions. My work employs a combination of machine learning, predictive modeling, statistical analysis, quantitative text analysis, natural language processing, large language models, and survey analysis.

In my book project, Local Peace Agreements in Civil Wars: Legitimacy, Violence, and Conflict Trajectories, I examine why non-state armed groups and communities negotiate and sign local agreements during ongoing wars and how these settlements shape intrastate conflict dynamics. Drawing on an original dynamic network dataset of local peace agreements in the Central African Republic, I develop a machine learning based prediction framework that demonstrate how these pacts recalibrate power balances, influence patterns of civilian victimization, and reshape interactions among armed actors.

I pursue related research on the political economy of conflict and security as part of my postdoctoral fellowship, where I am involved in the GRIER project and apply large language models and machine learning to study international economic restrictions.

I co-organize the Violence, Instability and Peace (VIP) Workshop.

I received my Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of Pittsburgh in 2025. Previously, I completed an M.A. in International Relations at Koç University and a B.A. in Political Science and International Relations at Boğaziçi University.
